Cox's Bazar

Cox's Bazar is a city, fishing port, tourism center, and district headquarters in southeastern Bangladesh. It is located 150 km (93 mi) south of Chittagong, and is the country's longest beach, stretching over 120 km (75 mi). The beach is a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year for its natural beauty and tropical climate. The city is also known for its unique culture, colorful markets, and friendly people. Additionally, Cox's Bazar is surrounded by hills and forests, providing opportunities for trekking and nature exploration. Overall, Cox's Bazar is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the natural and cultural wonders of Bangladesh.

Saint Martin

Saint Martin's Island is a small island located in the northeastern part of the Bay of Bengal, off the coast of Bangladesh. It is the only coral island in Bangladesh and is considered one of the country's most important tourist destinations due to its natural beauty and unique ecosystem. The island is home to a variety of wildlife, including exotic birds, reptiles, and sea creatures, making it a popular destination for bird watching and snorkeling. Saint Martin's Island can only be reached by boat, and visitors can enjoy the island's white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and lush vegetation. The island is also a popular destination for fishing and boating activities, and offers a peaceful and serene escape from the bustling mainland. Overall, Saint Martin's Island is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in experiencing the beauty of Bangladesh's coastline and marine environment.
